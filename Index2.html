Stuff That Lights My Brain Up

  • Technology and computers — especially anything that blinks, beeps, or buzzes. I love understanding systems and figuring out how things work.
  • Tech experiments and projects — things like building firewalls, experimenting with I2P networks, running Freenet servers, building Magic Mirror displays, flashing routers, and occasionally flashing phones just to see what I can make them do.
  • Questions — I absolutely love asking questions about everything. In college one of my teachers nicknamed me “One More Question.” If I had a superpower, it would probably be my ability to ask questions all day, every day.
  • Legos — My favorite toy in the world. When I moved back to Washington I discovered I had seven boxes of stuff, and two of them were just Legos.
  • Quiet walks in nature — especially peaceful places where you can think or have a great conversation.
  • Helping people solve tech problems — fixing something that’s frustrating someone else is very satisfying.
  • Research rabbit holes — if a topic catches my curiosity, I can go deep.
  • Trying new foods — The New Jeff is exploring more things the old Jeff wouldn’t try. Recent discovery: Thai Tea is amazing.
  • Comfort shows and anime — I rewatch shows like The Big Bang Theory, How I Met Your Mother, Friends, and The Flash. For anime, Yu Yu Hakusho, Soul Eater, and One Piece are some of my all-time favorites.
  • Video games — I enjoy Subnautica, Minecraft, Fortnite, open-world games, and I’m very good at Mario Kart.
  • Music — The angrier the rap, the more I usually like it. I also really enjoy songs I can sing along with, especially from the 80s, 90s, and 2000s.
  • Deep conversations — the kind where you really get to know someone and talk about how life actually works.

Core Jeff Values

  • Honesty — I believe being truthful and real with people builds the strongest relationships.
  • Optimism — Life can be difficult, but I try to always look for the win somewhere in the situation.
  • Gratitude — I believe people should appreciate both what they have and the things others do for them. A sincere “thank you” can never be said too often.
  • Kindness — Treating people with compassion and respect should always be the default.
  • Patience — Growth, healing, and learning all take time.
  • Unconditional Love — The people in my life matter deeply to me, and I try to care about them without expecting perfection.

My Red Flags

  • Trust can be hard for me. If trust is broken, it can take a long time for me to rebuild it.
  • Jealousy can happen sometimes. When I care about someone deeply, I can occasionally feel protective or insecure.
  • My emotions run big. I feel things strongly, which can sometimes make situations feel intense.
  • I sometimes overthink things. My brain likes to analyze situations and conversations a lot.
  • I’m physically affectionate. I’m a pretty touchy-feely person, which some people really like and others might not.
  • I can be picky about food. Although the New Jeff is actively expanding his food universe.
  • I like attention. I really enjoy engaged one-on-one interaction, and I find it rude when someone is glued to their phone while we’re spending time together.
  • When I care, I care a lot. My loyalty and emotional investment can be intense.
