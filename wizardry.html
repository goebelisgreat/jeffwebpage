Jeff Lore

Early Life

Learning Years
A long stretch of figuring out people, life, and how to navigate the world without spontaneously combusting.

Tech Discovery
At some point it became very clear that solving tech problems and helping people fix things was not only useful, but actually fun.

IT Career Arc
Years of learning systems, solving problems, and helping people who just want their computers to cooperate for five minutes.

The New Jeff Era
After the end of a 16-year relationship that shaped a huge part of my life, I entered what I call the New Jeff Era. This chapter is about rebuilding, rediscovering who I am as an individual, trying new things, and building a life that feels exciting and meaningful again.

Stuff That Lights My Brain Up

  • Technology and computers — especially anything that blinks, beeps, or buzzes. I like understanding systems and making them behave.
  • Tech experiments — firewalls, weird network projects, Magic Mirrors, flashing routers, and the occasional “let’s see if I can make this work” idea.
  • Questions — I genuinely love asking them. In college, one teacher nicknamed me “One More Question.”
  • LEGOs — still one of my favorite things on earth.
  • Quiet walks in nature — especially places where you can think, talk, or just breathe for a minute.
  • Helping people solve problems — fixing something that’s frustrating someone else is deeply satisfying.
  • Research rabbit holes — if something catches my curiosity, I will absolutely go too deep.
  • Trying new foods — the New Jeff is much more willing to explore than the old one was.
  • Comfort shows and anime — rewatching favorites counts as self-care.
  • Video games — especially games that let me explore, build, or get a little lost in them.
  • Music — angry rap, sing-along songs, and a lot of 80s, 90s, and 2000s energy.
  • Deep conversations — the kind where you actually learn who somebody is.

Core Jeff Values

  • Honesty — I believe truthful, real conversations build the strongest relationships.
  • Optimism — life can be messy, but I still try to look for the win.
  • Gratitude — appreciation matters, and “thank you” should never be rare.
  • Kindness — compassion and respect should be the default setting.
  • Patience — growth, healing, and learning all take time.
  • Loyalty — the people in my life matter deeply to me.

My Green Flags

  • I work on myself. Personal growth matters to me.
  • I care deeply. When I’m in, I’m really in.
  • I show up for people. If someone I care about needs help, I’ll be there.
  • I listen well. I like real conversations, not just waiting for my turn to talk.
  • I’m curious. I enjoy learning how things and people work.
  • I communicate honestly. I’d rather have a real conversation than a fake smooth one.
  • I’m supportive. I want the people around me to feel safe, understood, and encouraged.

My Red Flags

  • Trust can be hard for me. Once it’s broken, rebuilding it can take a long time.
  • I overthink. My brain likes to replay conversations and analyze details.
  • My emotions run big. I feel things strongly, which can make life feel intense sometimes.
  • I can get jealous or insecure. Usually when I care a lot.
  • I’m physically affectionate. Some people love that, some people absolutely do not.
  • I like engaged attention. If we’re spending time together and you’re glued to your phone, I’m probably going to hate that.
  • When I care, I care a lot. My loyalty can be a lot of energy in one place.

Jeff Connection Settings

  • Primary Love Language: Physical Touch
    Physical touch is easily my strongest love language. Things like hugs, sitting close, casual affection, or a hand on the shoulder make me feel connected and grounded with someone.
  • Conversation Mode
    My second biggest way of connecting with people is through deep one-on-one conversations. I love getting into real discussions about ideas, life, philosophy, or anything interesting enough to explore.
  • Intellectual Sparring
    I genuinely enjoy thoughtful debates and verbal sparring. Not arguments — more like two people playfully testing ideas, perspectives, and logic. Seeing how someone thinks and how sharp they are is incredibly attractive to me.
  • Bonus Feature
    Back scratches are basically a cheat code. If someone scratches my back, I will probably melt on the spot.

How to Unlock Jeff Friendship Mode

  • Be real. I connect best through honest conversations and genuine energy.
  • Show that you care. Feeling cared for means a lot to me.
  • Bring curiosity. I get along best with people who like ideas, learning, and growth.
  • Enjoy deep conversations. Small talk is fine, but real talk is where I connect.
  • Be present. Full attention goes a long way with me.
  • Kind words matter. Encouragement, compliments, and appreciation stick with me.
  • Trust me with real things. I value when people open up and let me know the actual them.
  • Know that I’m loyal. I connect best one-on-one, I joke around a lot, and if you’re my person, I’ll show up.

Staying Connected

  • I send regular updates to friends and family about what’s going on in my life.
  • I usually send daily, biweekly, and monthly messages so people stay in the loop.
  • It’s my way of sharing life updates without disappearing into the void.
  • I believe relationships stay stronger when people stay connected.

Little Weird Jeff Facts

  • I can get weirdly excited about solving small technical problems.
  • I like understanding how systems work — computers, networks, people, or life in general.
  • I occasionally build strange little tech experiments just to see if I can make them work.
  • If something is broken, confusing, or mysterious, my brain wants to poke at it until it makes sense.
  • I can turn a simple question into a full curiosity adventure with almost no warning.
